Q(qualcrim):You may qualify as indigent and be eligible for a court appointed attorney. In order for the court to determine whether you qualify, you will need to sign an affidavit that contains sufficient information for the court to ascertain your assets and liabilities.  Such information will typically include your income, property, and expenses, for example. See <a href="http://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ilcs/fulltext.asp?DocName=072500050K113-3" target=_new>725 ILCS 5/113-3(b)</a>.  If the court appoints you counsel, you may be ordered to pay a reasonable sum to reimburse either the county or the state for your representation.
